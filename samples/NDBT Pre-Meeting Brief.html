  • Regulatory weather is calm but watchful: Fed enforcement actions against small banks continue at a steady clip; FDIC proposing an overhaul of confidential information disclosure rules; stress tests passed cleanly at the large-bank tier. (Federal Reserve 7/2, FDIC 6/26)

Approach Angles

1. The independence play. Their nearest peer just sold to an out-of-state buyer. NDBT has stayed independent for 65 years and is expanding while others sell. Frame anything you offer as strengthening that independence — capability without acquisition. Never imply they're a target.
2. The Frisco expansion. The Fields banking center is their visible bet. New locations mean new operational load, new customer acquisition, new local competition (every bank wants Frisco). Ask what the expansion needs that the existing five centers didn't.
3. The disruption window. Dallas Capital Bank's customers and bankers are in play during the MidFirst transition — NDBT is a natural landing spot. If your offering helps them capture switchers (onboarding capacity, outreach, service speed), this is the month to say so.

Conversation Hooks

  • "Congratulations on Fields — how's the Frisco buildout going?" (their newest, proudest thing)
  • "What did you make of the Dallas Capital deal?" (invites them to talk positioning; listen for whether they see opportunity or threat)
  • The youth banking program — signals they're thinking in decades, not quarters. Match that horizon in your pitch.
